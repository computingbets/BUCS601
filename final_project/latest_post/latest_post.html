  • I can’t think about landscape design/architecture and NOT think about my family’s home. My mom bought it in the 80s, I grew up in it, and my dad still lives there. One of my mom’s big projects in regards to the house/grounds was the backyard garden, which blossoms in the spring and is absolutely beautiful. She—ok, professionals did the work and she merely supervised—built a 50-foot stone wall that wraps around the backyard. There’s a set of steps on one side that leads up the hill (hence the “Sasco Hill” in my family’s address) to our mailbox (a row of mailboxes, technically — it’s a private road). The garden extends the entire length of the wall, which curves in a semi-circle around the house. The house itself is a colonial, um, mansion built in 1929. It has huge Doric columns in the front — I like their simple design and clean lines.
